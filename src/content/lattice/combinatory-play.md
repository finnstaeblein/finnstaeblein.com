Load books as a cross-referenced lattice of mental models and let an LLM combine them: have one thinker critique another's framework, or fuse two frameworks into a third. The value isn't the summary of any single book — it's the non-obvious combinations that fall out when you force ideas from different domains against each other.

**Why it matters:** most originality is recombination. Tooling that surfaces cross-domain links turns reading into an idea multiplier instead of a stack of isolated notes.
