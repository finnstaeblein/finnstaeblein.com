Generative models produce enormous volumes of output — much of it plausible but wrong. The complementary function decides which outputs actually count: what to trust, accept, and ship. Relative to the money and attention pouring into generation, that acceptance layer is comparatively underbuilt.

**Why it matters:** as generation gets cheap and noisy, the scarce, valuable capability becomes judgment about output — deciding what's true and what to rely on.
