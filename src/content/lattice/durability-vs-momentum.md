A lens for evaluating a round: separate how *durable* the business is — will it still matter and keep compounding years out? — from how much *momentum* it has right now: hype, competitive heat, round velocity. You're hunting for durability. Ideally you find it paired with *low* momentum, because a quiet, under-hyped round tends to price cheaper — so the same durability costs you less to own.

**Why it matters:** momentum is what everyone bids up; durability is what actually pays off. Buying durability *before* the momentum arrives is where the return lives.
